About CSE

"SCE" can refer to several different things depending on the context, including Southern California Edison, a major utility company; Supply Chain Execution, a set of applications for managing logistics; Safety Critical Elements, a risk management concept in industrial safety; and Specification Common Elements, a technical standard for software specifications. It can also stand for Scottish Certificate of Education in the UK or be a shorthand for various other organizations and initiatives.

Southern California Edison (SCE)
  • One of the largest investor-owned electric utilities in the United States, serving Southern California.
  • Provides electricity and works on issues like solar energy credits, disaster recovery, and customer assistance, notes SCE.

Supply Chain Execution (SCE)
  • Refers to the execution-oriented applications that manage day-to-day logistics operations.
  • Includes systems like Warehouse Management Systems (WMS), Transportation Management Systems (TMS), and Global Trade Management (GTM) systems, according to Gartner.
